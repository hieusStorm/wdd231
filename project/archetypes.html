Arechtypes

What is an archetype? In Magic: The Gathering, an archetype refers to the overarching strategy that a deck employs. It serves as a framework that guides card selection and influences why specific cards are included. By defining the deck’s playstyle, archetypes help players understand their strategy and the optimal approach to achieving victory.

Aggro

Aggro decks are designed to win games quickly, focusing on fast-paced offense. They typically achieve this by aggressively attacking with creatures or employing low-cost spells to deal direct damage to the opponent.

Control

Control decks are crafted to secure victory by first establishing dominance over the game and preventing opponents from gaining a foothold. Typically, these decks operate in two phases: the control phase and the finisher phase. In the control phase, players utilize various forms of removal and disruption to stifle the opponent’s strategy and prevent any significant advantages. Once control is firmly established, the deck transitions to the finisher phase, where victory is achieved through leveraging board advantage and deploying powerful threats.

Combo

Combo decks aim to secure victory through specific combinations of cards. Typically, this is an A+B approach, where playing card A alongside card B leads directly to winning the game. Occasionally, combo decks are built around an engine—a set of cards designed to generate ongoing value or facilitate a win condition. While these engines may not end the game immediately, their repeated use can ultimately lead to a decisive victory.

Tempo

Tempo decks aim to win by blending aggressive and control strategies. Typically featuring low-cost creatures, these decks switch between an aggressive play style and a more controlling approach. Tempo decks focus on setting the pace of the game, using fast, efficient threats and strategic interactions to maintain an advantage. By applying pressure while also disrupting their opponent’s game plan, they create opportunities to secure victory.

Midrange

Midrange decks serve as a balanced approach between control and combo strategies. They incorporate both explosive win conditions and slower, interactive cards, allowing for versatility in gameplay. These decks can adapt their main strategy based on the current game state, shifting focus to whichever style—aggressive or controlling—best suits the situation. This flexibility enables midrange decks to respond effectively to various opponents and game dynamics.
